Version:      1 (+)
Your first Navi begins at Version 1.
In the MMM, a navi's version often describes their power. It's a notation of progress, and a badge of honor. In many other places, this would be your equivalent level, but this is not other places... the Version represents a little more than statistical progress, such as your measured power in Roleplay and, sometimes, the number of iterations your navi has gone through to reach the point they're at today.

Experience:  500 Exp (+)
You begin with 500 Exp.
The amount of Experience you obtain determines your Version. To obtain EXP, participate with an approved navi in roleplay, battles, events, and parties.

Funds:
Zenny:          z (+)
Your first Navi begins with 1000 Zenny.
Zenny is the primary coin that purchases most programs you will ever use. Additional Navis do not begin with any Zenny. Zenny cannot be shared between Navis except through savings accounts in the Electopian First International Bank.
Bugfrags:     0 (+)
You start off with 0 BugFrags.
BugFrags are non-tradable fragments of Bug Data that can be turned in for powerful items and tokens which can increase your other earnings. They are most commonly earned by accomplishing Achievements.

Type:             (+)
Choose 1 Type.
Your navi's Type is a representation of their combat philosophy and determines how they grow with every Version-up. Ofttimes they impart some unique attribute unavailable to any other Navi type. Some may modify your starting stats. At Version 1, you must select a single Type from the list in the link above and gain all bonuses not limited to Version. Note that you may never naturally obtain a second Type.

Karma:          , place holder (+)
You begin with 0 Karma.
Karma is an alignment system that separates the good from the bad from the neutral. What a navi chooses to do in regards to morality over the course of Roleplay and battle changes their capabilities and their access to powerful abilities.
Karma ranges on a scale from -100 to +100.

Your Navi can gain +1 Karma at the end of battle per turn that the battle last.
This is optional, and can result in a max of +5 Karma per battle.
At +50 Karma, you are considered 'Light Karma'.
Your Karma can only be raised higher through RP in Story Mode. Special Property: Hyper Mode
Light Karma (+)
A navi of Light Karma is the pinnacle of righteousness and good. Those with high karma are rewarded with advantages of greater versatility in the form of a significant number of Hyper Attacks and Hyper Modes which allow them to augment their fighting styles with potent finishers and impressive powers.


Your Navi will lose Karma every time they use a Dark Chip.
At -50 Karma, you are considered 'Dark Karma'.
Your Karma can only be dropped lower through RP in Story Mode.
Dark Karma (+)
A navi of Dark Karma is a force of evil and power. Dark Navis make use of Dark Chips, a set of very powerful single-use attacks, and they also gain the ability to use the Dark Soul: a pseudo-revival effect that provides temporary invulnerability to most forms of attack.


At Version 1, your Karma begins at 0 unless you have the Light or Dark Elements.
As long as your Karma remains between -49 and +49, you are considered 'Neutral Karma'.
Neutral Karma (+)
A navi of Neutral Karma is a source of balance and impartiality. Those of neutral karma have greater innate capabilities and higher general powers than those of extreme alignments. That is, they tend to have enhanced capabilities without drawing from many outside forces.

Special Property: Neutral Alignment (+)
Passive Effect: User has +1 Skill.

-Navi is invincible except against attacks modified by Aiming skills
-Navi has +1 Attack Slot while in Dark Soul
-Dark Soul lasts for 2 Turns

Elements are a core feature of almost every navi: They determine how people react to your attacks, how you deal with your opponent's attacks, and grant you various abilities related to them.
Each element has 5 levels of power. When you first obtain an element, whether through making your character or via an auxiliary system, that element begins at Level 1. When you gain additional element levels (or decide to designate more than one to an element upon navi creation), it graduates to the next subsequent level.

Skills are the tools with which you persevere and dominate in the Melee. Each skillset is, then, a toolbox crafted by the player and is in their complete control. You may never have 2 or more of the same skill. If you have a skill that dodges 2 attacks, then no matter what the additions to it you may never have another skill that dodges two attacks.

Skill Classification

- Activated Skills: Some skills are activated and have a limited number of times they can be used based on your Version and the power of the skill - these numbers may be augmented in or out of battle by other effects. Some skills even have hard caps, where regardless of effects they can only be used a certain number of times per battle.

- Passive Skills: Passive skills are weaker than activated skills, but they are always in effect and must be denoted as such.

- Custom Skills: Since skills are in your control, they may be capable of things that were not preset in our own guidelines. Some Skills may burn a unique resource, they may cost 2 Skills to create, or they may interact in such a way that classifies them as something totally different. The power is in your hands.

Skill Types

- Defense Skills primarily focus on mitigating damage, removing harmful statuses, and building your navi's durability.

- Speed Skills are designed to dodge incoming attacks, speed up your navi, modify the terrain, or build your navi's mobility.

- Support Skills provide protection with protective barriers and auras while also enhancing yours and you allies' survivability.

- Offense Skills add destructive power to your attacks through means of raw power, increased accuracy, or through some other method that essentially boosts your navi's damage output.

You begin with 5 Attacks. Their combined Cooldown cannot exceed your maximum CD.
You learn two new attacks at every Version-Up.

Your Attacks are often the core feature of your navi. They are a primary and dependable source of damage and allow for a level of customization second only to your Skills.

There are two possible Ranges for attacks: These are "C" (Combat), and "S" (Shot). Each attack must have a range chosen on creation. The range is both a role-play descriptor that tells you generally how close or far you are when you unleash the attack, and it's an interactive attribute for you to work with in your stats.

The Element of an attack determines several interactions that the attack has throughout the MMM battle system, such as the Strengths and Weaknesses of other Navis. When you create an attack, you must choose (from your navi's elements) which element your attack is.

By default, all of your attacks will deal damage equal to your navi's Base Damage. Adding Effects, Cooldown, and Drawbacks to an attack will raise or lower its damage accordingly. You cannot add effects or stack statuses on a navi such that the attack's damage (as calculated) is below 0

Basic Effects

Aimers are effects that boost the accuracy of your attack. The more you have, the more likely your attack will hit its target.

- Aimers cost 20 Damage to add to an attack, and you can never have more than 2 on the same attack.

- There are three kinds of aimers: Undodgeable, Breaks Shields, and Cuts Auras.

- Undodgeable means that the attack cannot be dodged or avoided with a "Dodge-Type" Speed Skill.

- Breaks Shields means that the attack cannot be blocked or avoided with a "Shield-Type" Defense Skill.

- Cuts Auras means that the attack cannot be stopped by a "Barrier" or "Aura"-Type Support Skill. This attack does damage to both the target and its Barrier/Aura, and will destroy it (the Barrier/Aura) if applicable.

Augments determine who, how, and when an attack hits or affects your opponents.

- The following effects fall under this category:
-- Cannot be Countered
-- Cannot be Intercepted
-- Ignores Armor
-- Hits All

- Augments cost 20 Damage to add to an attack and require at least 1 Turn of CD.

- You can have any number of Augments on the same attack, but you can only have 1 of each kind throughout your entire attack set per 8 Max CD your Navi has.

Advanced Effects have strong restrictions for use.

- The following effects fall under this category:
-- Destroys (Choose One: Objects | Clones | Barriers | Auras)
-- If Avoided: (followed by a triggerable effect)
-- Regardless of Success: (followed by a triggerable effect)
-- Avoid Separately (Applied to Multi Hit Attacks. Max: 4 Hits.)

- These effects cost 50 Damage to add to an attack and require at least 2 Turns of CD.

- The Cooldown of 'Destroys' attacks cannot be reduced below 1 Turn; this is their Mandatory Cooldown.

- You can have any number of these effects on the same attack, but you can only have 1 of each kind throughout your entire attack set per 12 Max CD your Navi has.

Restricted Effects cannot be used at all unless specifically granted by an outside source.

- The following effects fall under this category:
-- Unavoidable
-- Cuts Undershirt
-- Cuts Darksoul

- These effects have their own unique costs and limitations that are explained when you receive them.

- These effects all have a Mandatory 1 Turn CD.

Cooldown and Charge

Cooldown is attached to an attack or ability to represent the number of turns you must wait before you using that ability again.

Minimum Cooldown represents the lowest possible Cooldown that certain effects are allowed to have (this is also called Required Cooldown or Mandatory Cooldown).

Cooldown is applied to attacks with powerful effects (to prevent spamming and abuse). As an added bonus, the attack in question will deal additional damage depending on how long its Cooldown is. If the Cooldown is higher than the Minimum Cooldown of that attack, it will deal even more damage.

1 CD: +10 Damage (+20 Damage if above minimum)
2 CD: +30 Damage (+50 Damage if above minimum)
3 CD: +50 Damage (+70 Damage if above minimum)
4 CD: +70 Damage (+100 Damage if above minimum)
5 CD: +100 Damage (+150 Damage if above minimum)

Charge is an alternative to Cooldown. For each turn of Charge on an attack, you must spend an attack slot for that many turns before you can use it.

Optional Charge is not an alternative to Cooldown. It enables you to choose whether or not you charge an attack before unleashing it. Optional Charges can be released anywhere between 1-5 Turns and have reduced Damage Multipliers, but otherwise follow the same rules as regular Charged Attacks.

Attacks with Optional Charge lose 10 Damage per turn they are capable of being charged.

Each Attack can only be charged once per turn. You may not have multiple instances of the same charge at once. Charges are not held indefinitely: you must state which attack you're charging every turn (and spend the Attack Slot to do so), otherwise the Charge is dropped. Likewise, you must release the Attack at the end of its Charge, otherwise the Charge is dropped.

Charged Attacks are released 1 Turn after their last charge. In exchange for their strict guidelines, Charged Attacks provide immense power depending on how long they've been held.

1 Charge: x1.75 Damage (x1.5 if Optional)
2 Charge: x3.00 Damage (x2.5 if Optional)
3 Charge: x4.25 Damage (x4.0 if Optional)
4 Charge: x5.50 Damage (x5.0 if Optional)
5 Charge: x6.25 Damage (x6.0 if Optional)
